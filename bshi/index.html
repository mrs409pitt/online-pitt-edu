Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the program fully online?

Yes. All coursework is delivered online and asynchronously meaning there are no required campus visits and no set login times. You complete assignments on your own schedule. A clinical internship is required but can be completed at an approved site near you.

What makes this program different from other health informatics degrees?

This is the nation's first CAHIIM-accredited online bachelor's degree in health informatics. The curriculum emphasizes AI, machine learning, and health data science taught by University of Pittsburgh faculty who are active researchers in these fields.

What prerequisites do I need?

You'll need approximately 60 completed college credits, including coursework in programming, statistics, math, and general education. Community college transfer credits are accepted. Contact an enrollment specialist for a personalized prerequisite evaluation.

What kind of jobs can I get with this degree?

Graduates are prepared for roles in health data analytics, clinical informatics, health IT project management, AI applications in health care, and health information management. On-campus graduates report a 94% employment rate with an average starting salary of $74,000.

How long does it take to complete the program?

Students entering with 60 prerequisite credits can complete the 60-credit major in 2 years (4 semesters) on a full-time basis. Part-time options are also available for those who need more flexibility.

What is CAHIIM accreditation and why does it matter?

CAHIIM (Commission on Accreditation for Health Informatics and Information Management Education) is the recognized accrediting body for health informatics programs. Graduating from a CAHIIM-accredited program is essential for professional certifications like the CHDA and signals quality to employers.
