Why It Works

An online RN-BSN Degree Completion Program Built Around Your Life as a Nurse.

From schedule to cost to curriculum, every aspect of the Pitt-Bradford RN-BSN was designed with working registered nurses in mind.

01

Your Schedule, Your Terms

Coursework is online and asynchronous; this program has no required login times and no campus visits. Complete coursework when it works around your UPMC shifts. The program includes in-person clinical requirements.

02

Efficient Path to Completion

Registered nurses with all necessary prerequisites can complete the program in as few as two semesters. Connect with us if you would like to consider a part-time courseload.

03

Your UPMC Benefit Does the Work

UPMC's tuition assistance benefit of up to $6,000 per year is paid directly to Pitt-Bradford on your behalf. Eligible students who start in the spring term and finish in the fall will be able to have the entire cost covered by the tuition benefit.

04

Advance Your Nursing Career

A BSN degree positions you for leadership roles, higher earning potential, advanced practice foundations, and expanded career pathways across the healthcare field.

05

The Pitt Name. The Pitt Credential.

Earn your degree from the University of Pittsburgh, one of the nation's top research universities. Learn from practicing nursing faculty who understand your world.

06

PA & Out-of-State: Same Rate

The UPMC Partner Rate is identical for eligible Pennsylvania and out-of-state students. There is no geographic penalty wherever you're based, you pay the same.

Financial Details

How Your UPMC Tuition Benefit Works with this Program.

UPMC makes the process straightforward. Here's how to access your benefit each term.

Note: Alternatively, you may pay Pitt Bradford directly and seek reimbursement from UPMC at the end of the term.

1

Submit a UPMC Tuition Advancement Application

At the start of each term, submit a tuition advancement application through UPMC's standard process.

2

Receive Your Advancement Letter

UPMC issues you an Advancement Letter confirming your approved benefit amount for the term.

3

Present the Letter to Pitt Bradford Financial Aid

Submit your Advancement Letter to Pitt Bradford's Financial Aid office. UPMC pays your tuition directly. No out-of-pocket payment required upfront.

4

Apply for Additional Aid (If Needed)

You may also apply for available federal, state, or institutional aid to cover any remaining balance beyond your UPMC benefit.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions.

Do I need to come to campus for any classes?

No. This Pitt-Bradford RN-BSN degree completion program is offered online with asynchronous courses. There are no required campus visits and no set login times. You can complete coursework on your own schedule. Three in-person clinical experiences are required.

How does UPMC's tuition benefit get applied?

At the start of each term, you submit a UPMC Tuition Advancement application. You'll receive an Advancement Letter, which you present to Pitt Bradford's Financial Aid office. UPMC pays your tuition directly to Pitt-Bradford — no out-of-pocket cost required upfront.

Can students that start in the spring really complete the program with no tuition cost?

Full-time students who begin in the spring term can access two UPMC tuition benefit cycles — one before August 1 and one after — which can potentially cover the full tuition cost of both terms at the UPMC Partner Rate.

What if I graduated from a UPMC School of Nursing?

UPMC nursing school graduates receive priority admission to RN-BSN programs at partner universities, making Pitt Bradford's program a natural, credentialed next step for graduates of any of the eight UPMC Schools of Nursing. Graduates need to meet all degree completion program prerequisites.

How long does it take to complete the program?

Registered nurses can complete the program in as few as two semesters. The program may also be completed on a part-time basis for those who prefer more flexibility. You will need to meet the prerequisites and admissions criteria. Connect with us to learn more.

Is the UPMC Partner Rate the same for out-of-state nurses?

Yes. The UPMC Partner Rate of $5,250 per term is identical for eligible Pennsylvania and out-of-state students. No geographic penalty applies.
