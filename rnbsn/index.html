Financial Details

How to Pay for Your Degree.

There are several ways to fund your RN-BSN. Here's how to explore your options and minimize out-of-pocket costs.

Note: Many healthcare employers offer tuition assistance or reimbursement programs. Check with your employer's HR or benefits office for details.

1

Check Your Employer's Tuition Benefits

Many healthcare employers offer tuition assistance, tuition reimbursement, or educational advancement programs. Contact your HR department to find out what's available to you.

2

Complete the FAFSA

Submit your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to determine your eligibility for federal grants, loans, and state financial aid programs.

3

Look Into Nursing Scholarships

Explore scholarships specifically for working RNs pursuing a BSN. Professional associations, foundations, and state programs often have funding set aside for nurses advancing their education.

4

Connect with Pitt-Bradford Financial Aid

Our financial aid office can help you build a personalized funding plan that combines employer benefits, federal aid, scholarships, and any other available resources.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions.

Do I need to come to campus for any classes?

No. This Pitt-Bradford RN-BSN degree completion program is offered online with asynchronous courses. There are no required campus visits and no set login times. You can complete coursework on your own schedule. Three in-person clinical experiences are required.

What financial aid is available?

Students may be eligible for federal and state financial aid by completing the FAFSA. Many employers also offer tuition assistance or reimbursement programs for nurses pursuing a BSN. Contact our enrollment team for help building a personalized funding plan.

Can my employer's tuition benefit cover the full cost?

Depending on your employer's benefit structure and how you time your enrollment, employer tuition assistance programs can significantly reduce or potentially cover your full tuition cost. An enrollment specialist can help you map out the details.

How long does it take to complete the program?

Registered nurses can complete the program in as few as two semesters. The program may also be completed on a part-time basis for those who prefer more flexibility. You will need to meet the prerequisites and admissions criteria. Connect with us to learn more.

What prerequisites do I need?

You'll need an active RN license, an ASN or nursing diploma, 24–30 prerequisite college-level credits, and a cumulative GPA of 2.5 or higher. If you're not sure whether your credits qualify, fill out the form and our team will help you assess your readiness.

Is this program available to out-of-state students?

Yes. Because this is a fully online program, students from outside Pennsylvania are welcome to apply. Contact our enrollment team for tuition details specific to your situation.
